CourtCraft.ai — "Research from Genomes" Complete Flow

What happens when you type a legal question and click the button — function by function, field by field

1
Query Expansion
Claude Haiku · ~2s

expand_query(question) → _call_haiku(QUERY_EXPANSION_PROMPT)

Your plain-English question gets transformed into structured search terms. Haiku reads the full taxonomy list (17 categories, 28 topics) and extracts 6 types of search signals.

Input: "Can a cheque bounce complaint be quashed under Section 482 CrPC?"

statutory_terms: ["cheque dishonour", "quashing"]
synonyms: ["setting aside", "striking down"]
provisions: ["Section 138 NI Act", "Section 482 CrPC"]
legal_concepts: ["inherent jurisdiction", "abuse of process"]
taxonomy_ids: ["CAT_NI_ACT", "TOP_NI138_QUASH"]
negative_keywords: ["motor accident", "land acquisition"]

Rule: Generic terms like "court", "judgment", "case" are excluded. Only issue-specific terms.

↓
All 6 signal types passed to scoring engine

AI Relevance Filter
Claude Opus 4.6 · Adaptive Thinking · ~60-90s

filter_relevant(question, candidates) → _call_opus(RELEVANCE_FILTER_PROMPT)

Opus 4.6 with adaptive thinking deeply analyzes each candidate. Writes a 2-3 sentence analysis BEFORE scoring (chain-of-thought). Uses a legal-specific rubric. Keeps only score ≥ 6.

What Opus sees per candidate:

TID
Title
Court
Provisions
Ratio (500 chars)
Summary (1000 chars)
DB Match Score + signals

Legal Rubric:

9-10 Directly on point (supporting OR opposing) · 7-8 Highly relevant · 5-6 Background · 3-4 Tangential · 1-2 Not relevant

Key rule: Contrary authority scores 8-10 (lawyers MUST know about opposing holdings)

Key rule: SC preferred over HC over Tribunals when relevance is equal

Relevant genomes (up to 15, sorted by relevance score) sent to report generator

5
Report Generation
Claude Sonnet 4 · max 8000 tokens · ~50s

generate_report(question, relevant_genomes) → _call_sonnet(REPORT_GENERATION_PROMPT)

Sonnet 4 receives the question + all genome data and writes a structured legal research report with 6 mandatory sections. It follows 8 critical rules including court hierarchy labeling and no hallucination.

Section 1: EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

3-5 sentences directly answering the question with specific case references.

Sources from: ratio_decidendi, genome_summary

Section 2: KEY LEGAL PRINCIPLES

Numbered list. Each principle cites exact case title + paragraph number from genome data. Labeled as SC or HC source.

Sources from: ratio_decidendi (proposition + source_paragraph), case_identity (court)

Section 3: SUPPORTING JUDGMENTS

Per-judgment breakdown: case title, court, year, BINDING / PERSUASIVE label, ratio decidendi, key paragraphs, how it supports the question.

Sources from: case_identity, ratio_decidendi, provisions_engaged, precedent_registry

Section 4: CONTRARY / DISTINGUISHABLE POSITIONS (CRITICAL)

Mandatory section. Must include opposing holdings, vulnerability_map weaknesses, and distinguishing factors. Cannot be omitted or dismissed with "no contrary authority."

Sources from: vulnerability_map (weak_point, stress_test), distinguishing_playbook, precedent_registry (DISTINGUISHED/OVERRULED)

Section 5: PRACTICAL APPLICATION

Actionable advice for the lawyer. Sword strategies (offensive use) and shield strategies (defensive use) with specific paragraph citations.

Sources from: sword_uses (how_to_cite, draft_argument, scenario), shield_uses (attack_being_faced, how_judgment_helps)

Section 6: STRENGTH ASSESSMENT

Rating: STRONG / MODERATE / WEAK with reasoning. Factors: SC authority count, contrary holdings, judgment recency.

Sources from: all genome data collectively, precedent_registry (treatment), case_identity (court, date)
